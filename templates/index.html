🧠 About Parkinson's Disease

Parkinson's is a progressive nervous system disorder that affects movement. Early symptoms include tremors, stiffness, and difficulty with coordination. Detecting it early can help in managing the condition better.

✅ What You Should Do

  • Consult a neurologist for proper diagnosis
  • Follow a healthy diet and exercise regularly
  • Keep track of motor symptoms and report changes

❌ What to Avoid

  • Avoid self-medication
  • Do not ignore early signs like tremors or typing difficulty
  • Avoid stressful environments when possible

❓ FAQs

Q: Is Parkinson's curable?
A: Currently, there's no cure, but treatments can improve quality of life.

Q: Can it be prevented?
A: While it's not preventable, a healthy lifestyle can reduce risk.

Q: How does typing pattern help?
A: Slower or inconsistent keystrokes can be early signs of motor decline.

Q: What age group is most affected?
A: Parkinson's typically affects people over 60, but early-onset Parkinson's can begin earlier.

Q: Are tremors always a sign of Parkinson’s?
A: Not always. Other conditions like essential tremor or medication side effects can also cause shaking.

Q: Can stress make symptoms worse?
A: Yes, stress can aggravate symptoms such as tremors and stiffness.

Q: Are there support groups available?
A: Yes, many local and online communities offer support, education, and social interaction for patients and caregivers.

Q: Does everyone with Parkinson’s have the same symptoms?
A: No. Symptoms vary greatly from person to person in severity and progression.
